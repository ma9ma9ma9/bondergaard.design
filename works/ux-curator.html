Overview

Keeping up with design news meant checking a dozen different sites, scrolling past ads and promotions, just to find a handful of articles worth reading. It was exhausting. So I built UX Curator to handle it for me.

UX Curator pulls from RSS feeds, scores articles by relevance, and sends me a curated digest three times a week. The AI does the filtering, I just read what makes the cut. It focuses on the latest trends in design, UX and HCI, and explains why each article matters for my work.

I also set up a website where people can sign up for the newsletter. This opens up the possibility of expanding beyond UX—letting users specify their interests and get curated emails based on what they care about.
